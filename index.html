My name is Travis Vesbach.

After graduating from university in 2014, I moved to Japan for a few years to teach English. During my time there, I studied Japanese, learned more about the world outside of the U.S., and learnted more about myself. Though I loved my time there, I decided to return in order to pursue a career in programming.

Since returning in May of 2017, I have been fully focused on studying by using The Odin Project as my guide. One of my most rewarding projects was making a Facebook clone using Ruby on Rails. More recently, I have been developing my JavaScript skills.

I believe that my hard-working and friendly personality, combined with my quick learning skills and nature to relentlessly face problems head on can allow me to become an asset to any development team. I am always interested in learning new languages and skills to help me become a better programmer.
